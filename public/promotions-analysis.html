Purpose of this document

Analysis of Cart Price Rules usage across all active Dr.Max markets, based on 2025 Magento order data. Collected via country workshops to capture the as-is status — how promotion rules are set up, used, and where the current solution falls short.

This document serves as the foundation for redesigning the Promotions Module — informing UX design decisions and the development team about current reality across markets.

Cart rules vs Item rules
🛒 Cart rules
Step counting and discount distribution based on the whole cart. Discount split proportionally among all items.
📦 Item rules
Step counting and discount applied per specified item independently. Discount on each matching product separately.

What Works Well

✅ Strengths of Current Solution12 / 15
🔧
Flexible rule engine
34 rule type combinations covering virtually all promotion scenarios. Qty, Spent, Realspent triggers × Cart/Item scope × multiple action types. 26 actively used — the engine is genuinely versatile.
🎟️
Coupon generation & export
Auto-generated unique codes with configurable length, format, prefix/suffix, and CSV export for call centers and partner distribution. Used actively in all 4 workshop markets.
📝
Customer-facing text fields
Promo title, promo text, and legal disclaimer fields embedded in the rule. All markets use these. Legal is mandatory in RO. Supports the full customer communication flow from within the promotion itself.
🏪
Channel targeting
Rules can target specific channels: web, mobile app, brick & mortar. PL uses shop_type conditions to differentiate pharmacy vs online promotions. IT uses Reservation Allowed for Click & Collect.
🔗
ERP integration (Farmis)
Farmis Voucher ID and Code fields are mandatory across all markets. Direct linkage to the ERP for accounting reconciliation is built into the rule, not bolted on.
⚙️
Rich condition system
18 distinct condition attributes used globally. Supports both quantifiable (≤ ≥ = range) and non-quantifiable (include/exclude/AND/OR) operators. Multiple conditions per rule with full logical grouping.
📅
Auto-apply with date validity
Rules auto-apply when conditions are met — no coupon code needed for standard promotions. Date-based from/to scheduling allows advance setup and automatic deactivation.
🛑
Global usage cap
Group ID Max Count and Global Max Usage fields provide budget protection — rules stop firing once a global usage limit is hit. IT and RO use this actively for managed campaign budgets.

Current Challenges & Workarounds

🔍 Identified Issues13 / 15
📊
Priority system is unreliable. Romania actively avoids it. Slovakia and Poland use it inconsistently. No confidence in execution order when multiple rules match — teams resort to workarounds instead of fixing priority config.
🎭
Romania: "fake rules" workaround. Rules with 0% discount are created purely to display promotional texts and labels, because advanced pricing limitations in Magento prevent attaching texts to real rules in certain configurations. Operational overhead and risk of confusion.
🧮
Missing VAT-inclusive subtotal condition (RO). The base_subtotal condition excludes VAT. Romania needs VAT-inclusive cart total for accurate rule triggering, requiring manual workarounds or accepting inaccurate thresholds.
🧹
No rule lifecycle governance. Romania has 1,769 gift_extra_empty_cart rules but only 1,644 corresponding orders — large number of stale, never-triggered rules accumulating. No tooling to identify or clean up unused rules.
🔢
High stacking risk in Poland. 37% of PL orders have 2+ rules applied. "Discard subsequent rules" is the only stacking control — a blunt on/off switch. No fine-grained combinability management or preview of what stacks with what.
🧠
Many admin fields are unused or misunderstood. Coupon EAN, Special event URL, Specific coupon SKU, RSS feed, Promo banners, Related dynamic blocks — none used across any market. High cognitive load on rule managers from irrelevant fields cluttering the UI.
👁️
No rule interaction visibility. Managers cannot see which rules will combine on a given cart before activation. Testing requires creating orders or relying on experience, not tooling.
📋
Customer groups underused. Limited to voucher scenarios in most markets. Full customer segmentation potential of the rule engine is not leveraged — partly due to complexity, partly due to unclear field behaviour.

Legal & ERP Constraints

⚖️ Compliance Requirements14 / 15
📌
These requirements are non-negotiable — they must be preserved in any redesign of the Promotions Module. The new system must continue to support all of these out of the box.
ERP Integration — mandatory all markets
🔗
Farmis Voucher ID + Code are mandatory in all 4 workshop markets (IT, RO, SK, PL). Every promotion rule must carry ERP identifiers for accounting reconciliation. Any redesign must preserve this link.
💳
Uses per coupon is a mandatory field in Romania, required for financial control. Other markets use it, but RO enforces it on every rule. Relates to budget/cost accounting for individual campaigns.
📤
Generate & export coupon codes is actively used in all markets — codes are distributed to call centers, partners, and loyalty programs. CSV export capability must be maintained.
🏪
Reservation Allowed is critical for Italy (Click & Collect / pharmacy reservation flow). Rules must be applicable to reservation orders, not just checkout purchases.
Legal requirements
📄
Promo legal text field is mandatory in Romania by local law — promotional text must include a legal disclaimer. All other markets use it as a standard practice. Must remain a first-class field.
🎯
Channel-specific validity is legally relevant in some markets — promotions valid only online vs. in-store vs. pharmacy must be correctly scoped. The "Valid in" channel restriction field is used in all markets.
🏷️
Action labels (product tiles) must display the correct promotion labelling on product pages. These are legally required to accurately represent the discount type being offered — not just decorative.
⚠️
Non-combinable rules for vouchers. Specific voucher promotions must be non-combinable with other rules by legal/commercial agreement. The current "Non-combinable sales rule" field handles this — must be preserved.
Fields that can be removed / simplified in redesign
RSS feed Coupon EAN (barcode) Special event URL Specific coupon SKU Coupon product quantity Coupon image uploader Promo banners section Labels (admin section) Related dynamic blocks Skip Gifts for Rule Calculation Fix Qty by Step Public in RSS feed
None of the above are used in any of the 4 workshop markets. Safe candidates for removal or deep-hide in the new UI.
